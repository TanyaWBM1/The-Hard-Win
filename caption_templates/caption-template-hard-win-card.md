# Caption Template — Hard Win Card

A reusable pattern for a main figure card caption. Adapt to the specific story; never force
the structure over the truth. Keep ≤5 hashtags, in the caption.

> Stub / living template.

---

## Structure
1. **Direct opening line** — the hook (what they did, the hard version).
2. **Context** — the historical/cultural situation, with real dates/specifics.
3. **The hard win** — the verifiable achievement.
4. **Grounded takeaway** — one honest, non-hype line.
5. **Receipt** — the source line ("Confirmed: …").
6. **Up to 5 hashtags** (see `HASHTAG_RULES.md`).

## Pattern
```text
[Direct opening line about the hard version of what they did.]

[2–4 plain sentences of real context: who they were, the barrier, the dated facts.]

The hard win: [the verifiable achievement, stated plainly].

Receipt: [fact + dates], confirmed across [Source A], [Source B].

Real people. Real proof.

#thehardwin #RealHistory #BlackHistory #[Person] #[Theme]
```

## Example
```text
She did not wait for permission.

Ida B. Wells documented lynching with data when newspapers looked away — reporting,
publishing, and organizing against it at the risk of her life.

The hard win: she turned careful evidence into a movement that could not be ignored.

Receipt: pioneering anti-lynching reporting, 1890s; co-founder of the NAACP, 1909 —
confirmed across the Library of Congress and Encyclopaedia Britannica.

Real people. Real proof.

#thehardwin #RealHistory #BlackHistory #IdaBWells #BlackJournalism
```
*(Illustrative pattern — verify every fact to the two-source rule before publishing.)*
