## Current platform facts (mirror — see playbook for the source of truth)
- **Max 5 hashtags per post/Reel** (Instagram hard cap, enforced since Dec 2025), counted
  **caption + comment combined**.
- **Placement: in the caption** (best practice). The first comment carries the motivational
  opener + follow CTA, **no tags**.

## How we pick them (method)
1. **True for that person** — accuracy is the brand. A tag never goes on a card it isn't real for.
2. **Mix of sizes** — a couple of broad tags + smaller/niche ones you can actually surface in.
3. **Branded tags** to build a home: `#thehardwin`, `#blackhistoryrevived`.
4. **Don't overstuff** — fewer, relevant tags beat a wall of them.

## The 5-tag structure
```text
#thehardwin
#RealHistory                ← or #wisdomfromthepast on non-history-era figures
#BlackHistory               ← ONLY when the subject genuinely is Black history
#[person]                   ← e.g. #IdaBWells
#[theme_or_field]           ← e.g. #BlackJournalism
```
- `#BlackHistory` and `#blackhistoryrevived` are **gated** to Black-history subjects only.
- `#forgottenfigures` for the genuinely lesser-known (not the famous).
- Rejected as inaccurate/low-value: `#HarlemRenaissance` on non-HR figures, `#importantpeople`,
  `#unknownfigures`.

## Don'ts
- No more than 5. No hashtag stuffing. No tags that aren't true for the subject.
- Don't bury tags in the first comment for "extra slots" — the cap is total; there are none.
