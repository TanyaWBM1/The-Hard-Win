# The Hard Win

### Real People · Real Proof

*Turning overlooked history into clear, inspiring, well-designed social content.*

</div>

---

**The Hard Win** is an inspirational history and culture content system built to uncover overlooked stories, create disciplined social posts, and maintain a living Instagram platform playbook for captions, hashtags, originality rules, and posting workflows.

This repo is the source of truth for the project's content standards, Instagram rules, caption discipline, hashtag limits, originality guidelines, and quarterly platform-review process.

---

## Purpose

The Hard Win exists to turn overlooked history into clear, inspiring, well-designed social content.

The goal is not to chase viral trends or produce shallow quote cards. The goal is to build a repeatable content system that highlights people, events, places, and cultural moments that deserve more attention.

The project focuses on:

* Inspirational history
* Black history and culture
* Overlooked figures and forgotten wins
* Practical resilience
* Original visual storytelling
* Instagram-ready educational content
* Platform-safe posting practices

The central idea is simple:

> Every post should teach something real, honor the story, and leave the audience with a hard-earned win.

## Core Content Standard

The Hard Win content should be:

* **Original** — not reposted, scraped, copied, or lightly remixed.
* **Historically respectful** — no flattening people into slogans.
* **Inspirational without being fake** — grounded in real struggle, real context, and real achievement.
* **Readable** — short enough for social media, strong enough to carry meaning.
* **Visually disciplined** — consistent layout, typography, spacing, and brand tone.
* **Platform-aware** — built for Instagram without surrendering the voice to the algorithm.

---

## Originality Standard

The Hard Win treats source material as raw material, not finished content.

A post should only be considered safe for publication when it adds meaningful original contribution.

Original contribution layers may include:

* Original research
* Original captioning
* Original written framing
* Custom visual composition
* Distinct Hard Win design treatment
* Source-aware context
* Public-domain image transformation
* Editorial interpretation
* Provenance or citation notes

Public-domain images may be used, but they must be materially transformed through The Hard Win's own research, design, captioning, and editorial framing.

A light overlay, repost, screenshot, watermark, simple crop, or low-effort edit is not enough.

## Hashtag Discipline

The Hard Win follows a controlled hashtag system.

Current working rule:

```text
Maximum 5 hashtags per Instagram post or Reel.
```

The preferred structure is:

```text
#thehardwin
#RealHistory
#BlackHistory
#[person_or_place]
#[theme_or_field]
```

Example:

```text
#thehardwin
#RealHistory
#BlackHistory
#IdaBWells
#BlackJournalism
```

Hashtags should support classification and discovery. They should not replace strong captions.

The caption should carry the keywords, story, and emotional hook — and the hashtags ride **in the caption**, not buried in the first comment for extra slots.

## Caption Standard

Captions should be clear, specific, and searchable.

A strong Hard Win caption usually includes:

1. A direct opening line
2. The historical or cultural context
3. The hard win
4. A grounded takeaway
5. Up to 5 relevant hashtags

Avoid:

* Generic motivation
* Vague inspiration
* Over-polished AI language
* Empty praise
* Clickbait
* Unsupported claims
* Hashtag stuffing

Preferred tone:

> Clear, grounded, respectful, and quietly powerful.

## Automation Philosophy

This repo supports automation, but it does not remove human judgment.

The Hard Win uses a **human-approved automation model**:

```text
Research → Draft → Review → Approve → Schedule → Publish → Track
```

Automation may help with:

* Finding niche angles
* Organizing research
* Drafting captions
* Preparing post records
* Checking hashtag counts
* Opening quarterly review issues
* Updating playbook reminders

Automation should not blindly publish content without review.

The project's automation rule:

> Automation can prepare the work. A human must protect the meaning.

---

## Quarterly Platform Review

Instagram changes its rules, recommendation systems, and best practices over time.

This repo is tied to an automated quarterly review (a scheduled cloud agent) that re-verifies the playbook against official sources and opens a pull request with any changes for human approval.

The review should check:

* Instagram hashtag limits
* Hashtag placement guidance
* Caption/search guidance
* Original content rules
* Recommendation eligibility
* Instagram Creators announcements
* Meta platform/API publishing rules
* Any changes affecting automated posting

The result of each review should be one of the following:

* No change needed
* Playbook needs update
* Posting workflow needs update
* Hashtag/content templates need update

Every review should update the changelog in `INSTAGRAM_PLAYBOOK.md`.

## Recommended Workflow

A basic Hard Win post workflow:

```text
1. Choose a figure, event, place, or theme.
2. Gather source notes.
3. Write the story angle.
4. Draft the caption.
5. Create or render the visual card.
6. Confirm originality standard.
7. Confirm hashtag count is 5 or fewer.
8. Confirm caption contains the key search terms.
9. Schedule or publish.
10. Track performance.
```

## Post Quality Checklist

Before publishing, confirm:

* [ ] The post is original or materially transformed.
* [ ] The story is historically respectful.
* [ ] The caption explains the "hard win."
* [ ] Claims are supportable.
* [ ] Hashtag count is 5 or fewer.
* [ ] Hashtags are in the caption, not buried for extra slots.
* [ ] The post does not rely on copied screenshots or reposted content.
* [ ] The design matches The Hard Win style.
* [ ] The content is useful even if it does not go viral.

## Source Discipline

When adding research or updating platform rules:

* Prefer official sources first.
* Use reputable secondary sources when official sources are unavailable.
* Record the date checked.
* Do not treat old platform advice as current.
* Do not copy source text into content without transformation.
* Do not make ownership, copyright, or legal claims beyond what the sources support.

For Instagram rules, always check official Meta, Instagram, or Instagram Creators sources when possible.

## Safety Notes

The Hard Win should avoid:

* Over-automating public posts

* Reducing real people to motivational props

Every story deserves care.
